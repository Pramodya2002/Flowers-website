  • Soil Preparation:
    • Before planting, assess your soil's texture and composition. Amend heavy clay soils with organic matter like compost to improve drainage, while adding mulch to sandy soils to enhance water retention.
    • Conduct a soil test to determine pH levels and nutrient deficiencies. Adjust soil pH as needed by incorporating lime for acidic soils or sulfur for alkaline soils.
      • TOP

  • Plant Selection:
    • Choose plants that thrive in your climate and growing conditions. Consider factors such as sunlight exposure, soil type, and water requirements when selecting varieties for your garden.
    • Incorporate a mix of annuals, perennials, and native plants to ensure continuous bloom and provide habitat for beneficial insects and wildlife.
      • TOP

  • Watering Practices:
    • Water deeply and infrequently to encourage deep root growth and drought tolerance in plants. Use soaker hoses or drip irrigation systems to deliver water directly to the root zone and minimize evaporation.
    • Water early in the morning to reduce fungal diseases and avoid watering foliage to prevent leaf spot and mold issues.
      • TOP

  • Pest Control:
    • Implement integrated pest management (IPM) strategies to control pests without harming beneficial insects. Monitor plants regularly for signs of pest infestations and intervene early with cultural or organic control methods.
    • Use natural predators like ladybugs and praying mantises to control aphids and other garden pests organically.
      • TOP

  • Seasonal Care:
    • Spring:Prepare beds by clearing debris, dividing perennials, and applying a balanced fertilizer to promote new growth.
    • Summer:Mulch garden beds to conserve moisture and suppress weeds. Deadhead flowers regularly to prolong bloom and maintain plant health.
    • Fall: Plant bulbs for spring blooms, prune shrubs, and clean up garden beds to prepare for winter.
    • Winter: Protect tender plants with mulch or row covers, and plan for next year's garden by reviewing successes and areas for improvement.
      • TOP

  • Companion Planting:
    • Take advantage of companion planting to maximize garden health and productivity. Pair plants that benefit each other by repelling pests, improving soil fertility, or providing shade and support.
