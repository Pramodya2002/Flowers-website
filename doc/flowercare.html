Flower Care Guide



Keep Your Flowers Fresh

Discover essential tips and techniques for maintaining the beauty and longevity of cut flowers, from preparation to preservation:


Cutting and Conditioning

  • Choose the Right Tools: Use sharp, clean scissors or floral shears to cut flower stems at a 45-degree angle. This ensures a clean cut that allows for better water absorption.
  • Timing:Harvest flowers in the early morning or late evening when they are well-hydrated and temperatures are cooler.
  • Water Temperature: Fill a clean vase with lukewarm water and add floral preservative to nourish flowers and inhibit bacterial growth.
  • Remove Foliage:Strip lower leaves from stems to prevent them from sitting in water, which can lead to bacterial growth and shorten vase life.

Arranging Flowers in Vases:

  • Create a Framework:Start with a focal flower (e.g., roses or lilies) and build around it with complementary blooms and foliage.
  • Cutting Heights:Trim stems to varying lengths for depth and dimension. Arrange taller flowers towards the center and shorter ones around the edges.
  • Balance and Symmetry:Aim for a balanced arrangement by distributing colors and textures evenly. Consider the vase shape and size to achieve proportional balance.

Preserving Flowers:

  • Drying Flowers: Preserve flowers by hanging them upside down in a dry, dark area for several weeks. Use silica gel or desiccants to speed up the drying process.
  • Pressing Flowers:Press flowers between heavy books or using a flower press to flatten and preserve them for crafts, cards, or framed displays.

Longevity Tips:

  • Change Water Regularly:Refresh vase water every 2-3 days to prevent bacterial buildup and maintain flower freshness.
  • Trim Stems: Recut flower stems every few days to remove any blocked or slimy sections and improve water uptake.
  • Avoid Direct Sunlight and Heat:Keep floral arrangements away from direct sunlight, drafts, and heat sources to prolong bloom life.


Special Care for Specific Flowers:

  • Roses:Remove thorns and outer petals for a clean appearance. Change water daily to extend vase life.
  • Tulips:Place tulips in shallow water to prevent stems from bending. Add a penny to the vase to keep tulips upright.
  • Orchids: Mist orchid blooms with water to maintain humidity. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Explore these flower care tips and techniques to enhance the beauty and longevity of your floral arrangements. Whether you're arranging fresh flowers in vases or preserving blooms for future use, proper care ensures that your flowers stay fresh and vibrant longer.
